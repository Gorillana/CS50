Reading List

CS Reading List

Here, I've included a list of books that I've read and have considered very healthy in the beginning of my computer science and personal development journey.

The following books range from helping focus to controlling your reactions to life's realities
  • Deep Work: Cal Newport explains how to approach work with a more singular focus.
  • Shallows: Nichola Carr shows how the internet is affecting how your brain processes information.
  • Your Erroneous Zones: Wayne Dyer provides a lot of sobering advice and perspective throughout this book.

These books provide insight on how to approach coding as a programmer
  • The Pragmatic Programmer: David Thomas and Andrew Hunt provide reasonable or "pragmatic" tips on how to approach the realm and practice of programming to those just beginning and those who've been at it for years.
  • Learning Python: Part of the O'Reilly programming book series authored by Mark Lutz. I've honestly gotten through about 200/1500 pages and started looking for other ways to learn the basics of Python as I felt like I needed to learn how to think like a programmer before attempting to program.
  • Think Python: How to Think Like a Computer Scientist (2nd Edition): Allen Downey's, essentially, shortened version of "Learning Python"
  • Art of Computer Programming(Volume 1-4) by donald E Knuth - appears more advanced and I'll try to get through volume one here soon enough.
  • Algorithms by Jeff Erickson
  • Clean Code by Robert C Martin

The following are books that I am currently reading and aim to finish:
  • The Mathematics of Secrets: Joshua Holden helps relay the history of cryptography and how it's evolved into the tool it is today.
  • Applied Cryptography by Bruce Schneier
  • Hacker's Delight by Henry S. Warren, Jr.
